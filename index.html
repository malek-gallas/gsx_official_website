    Skateboarding is a popular sport where riders perform tricks on a board with wheels. It originated in California and has evolved into a global phenomenon.
    Skateboarding involves techniques like ollies, flips, and grinds, showcasing creativity and control. It has its own subculture and promotes self-expression and physical fitness.

    Whether for recreation or transportation, skateboarding offers an exhilarating experience for riders of all levels. Skateboarding has developed its own unique subculture that extends beyond the sport itself.
    Skateboarding culture encompasses a distinct fashion sense, music preferences, artistic expressions, and a strong sense of community.



    Aggressive inline skating, also known as rollerblading, is an action-packed sport that involves performing tricks and stunts on inline skates.
    It emerged in the late 1980s and gained popularity in the 1990s as a spin-off of roller skating. In aggressive inline skating, riders use specially designed inline skates with grind plates and smaller wheels for enhanced maneuverability.

    The sport combines elements of street skating, skateboarding, and BMX biking, incorporating jumps, grinds, slides, spins, and flips. Aggressive inline skating has its own vibrant and diverse culture.
    Skaters often form tight-knit communities, sharing their passion and skills through local scenes, events, and online platforms.
    These communities create a supportive environment where skaters can learn from and inspire one another.



    Freestyle scootering, also known as scootering or trick scooter riding, is a popular action sport that involves performing tricks and stunts on a specially designed scooter. It has gained significant traction in recent years and has developed its own unique culture and community.

    A freestyle scooter typically consists of a reinforced deck, handlebars, and wheels designed for durability and maneuverability. Scooter riders perform a wide variety of tricks, including jumps, spins, grinds, manuals, and flips, using skate parks, street spots, and specialized scooter parks as their playground.
    Freestyle scootering culture has a strong emphasis on creativity, progression, and camaraderie. Scooter riders often gather at skate parks or designated spots to ride together, share tricks, and push each other to new heights.
    This sense of community fosters a supportive and inclusive atmosphere where riders of all skill levels can learn and improve.



    Street art, also known as urban art or graffiti art, is a form of artistic expression that is created in public spaces, typically using various techniques such as spray paint, stencils, stickers, posters, and installations.
    It emerged as a subculture and artistic movement in the late 20th century and has since gained recognition and popularity around the world.

    Street art is often associated with the urban environment and serves as a means for artists to communicate their messages, challenge social norms, and reclaim public spaces.
    It can take many forms, including murals, graffiti tags, stencil art, wheat-pasting, and 3D installations. Artists use these mediums to express their creativity, make political or social statements, or simply add color and vibrancy to urban landscapes.

    Our Humble Story

    GSX (Génération Sports X-trêmes) is the first Tunisian association dedicated to extreme sports. Founded in July 2004, GSX brings together individuals who crave the thrill of pushing their limits and exploring new horizons. Our association was born from the vision of Mr. Tarek Hassan, who passionately believed in the power of extreme sports to inspire creativity and personal growth.

    Located in Hammam-Sousse, Tunisia, GSX is a hub for adrenaline enthusiasts who share a passion for extreme sports. We celebrate the thrill of high-speed skateboarding, the artistry of aggressive inline skating, and the fearless tricks of freestyle scooter riders. With a spirit of adventure and relentless determination, GSX fosters an environment where individuals come together, exchange ideas, and push their limits. Led by President Assem Djdidi, our dedicated team, along with partners like Make Life Skate Life and the Decathlon Foundation, is turning our dreams into reality. Join us at GSX and experience the exhilarating world of extreme sports.
